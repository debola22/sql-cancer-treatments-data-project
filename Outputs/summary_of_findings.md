## DATASET OVERVIEW
## The dataset contains 1,000 patient records from multiple countries. 
There were no duplicate patient IDs.
There were no missing values.

## DEMOGRAPHICS
Most patients were between 20-84 years old, with a median age of 50.
Males represented 51% of cases.
The dataset was almost dominated by patients from Africa, making up 50.1%.

## CANCER TYPES
Cervical cancer was the most common cancer type (12.3%), followed by Colon cancer (11.8%) and Skin cancer (11.8%)
These three cancers alone accounted for 35% of the dataset.

## STAGE DISTRIBUTION
Stage II and IV were the most common stages at diagnosis.
This suggests many patients present at intermediate and final stages rather than early detection.

## TREATMENT AND OUTCOMES
The overall recovery rate was 29.3%.
Patients with final stage cancers (Stage IV) had significantly higher recovery rates compared to early-stage cancers.
Hormone therapy had the highest recovery success rate among the treatment type.

## TREATMENT DURATION
The average treatment duration was 140 days.

## INCOME INSIGHTS
- The median income was 29,908.
- No outliers found.

## PROJECT LIMITATIONS
- The dataset did not fully represent many Africans countries.
